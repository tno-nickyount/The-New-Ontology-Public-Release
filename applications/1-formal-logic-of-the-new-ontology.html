Formal Logic and the New Ontology: Toward a Structural System

A dual-sorted logic model for capturing force, meaning, and resolution in the Master Domain.

Introduction

The New Ontology asserts that reality is a dual-domain structure in which all intelligible phenomena instantiate through force in the Empirical Domain and through coherence in the Aetherial Domain.

To formalize this structure, we outline a minimum viable product (MVP) for a formal logic system capable of representing and evaluating structural presence, identity, and collapse.

This supplement introduces the syntax, semantics, rules, and operations necessary to begin modeling the New Ontology computationally and conceptually.

Dual-Sorted Syntax

Our formal system operates with two distinct syntactic layers:

  • Empirical Terms represent Objects—physical entities, governed by force. These terms include variables, predicates, and operations corresponding to mass, motion, and spatial configuration.
  • Aetherial Terms represent Forms—semantic structures, governed by coherence. These terms model meaning, alignment, contradiction, and structural fidelity.

Each syntactic layer is governed by its own rules, akin to multi-sorted logic or typed formal systems. Together, they allow us to model both presence and intelligibility, across both domains.

Interpretation Functions

  • Empirical Interpretation maps empirical terms to structures governed by physical law—measurable, causal, and extended.
  • Aetherial Interpretation maps Forms to a logic of structural consistency—noncontradiction, alignment, and recursive resolution.

These interpretations ensure domain fidelity: that empirical terms preserve measurement-based relations, and that Aetherial terms do not instantiate if they result in contradiction.

A mapping function enables translation between domains—e.g., identifying the Form a physical Object corresponds to, or evaluating whether an action coheres with a resolved moral structure.

Inference and Proof

Empirical Inference

  • Operates on classical rules (e.g., modus ponens, conjunction introduction)
  • Allows derivation of outcomes based on material properties and causal dynamics

Aetherial Inference

  • Requires resolution rules:
    • If a structure is internally coherent, the Form may instantiate.
    • If contradiction is detected, the structure collapses.

This system affirms that even formal systems are approximations. But within these constraints, we can still model structural persistence, failure, and obligation with rigor.

Order of Description

  • Meta: general structural axioms
  • Macro: domain-specific patterns and laws
  • Micro: individuated configurations

This stratification affirms the however defined principle—precision is always contingent on contextual necessity, not ontological totality.

Domain Mapping

A critical operation is the mapping from Objects to Forms:

  • From measurable behavior to resolved meaning
  • From causal aggregation to semantic identification

Example: A physical tool becomes a hammer not by mass or shape alone, but by matching the resolved Form of "that which answers to hammer."

Mapping allows the system to model dual instantiation, and test coherence across domains.

Resolution and Collapse

All modeled structures must either:

  • Resolve: instantiate with internal coherence, or
  • Collapse: fail to instantiate due to contradiction

This applies across systems:

  • To concepts
  • To selves
  • To models
  • To ethical actions

Collapse is not failure to predict—it is failure to cohere.

Fidelity Metrics

A structural logic system allows formal evaluation of:

  • Fidelity to Form: how well a structure preserves coherence
  • F/S Ratio: fidelity relative to structural complexity

These metrics allow diagnostic and comparative evaluation of theories, models, identities, or agents. They also open the door to coherence testing in AI, cognition, or ethics.

Applications

Predictive Modeling

Integrate structural resolution into scientific models to assess both physical accuracy and semantic intelligibility.

Philosophy of Mind

Model experience as recursive resolution across time. Consciousness becomes measurable not by output, but by depth of coherence.

Ethics

Wrong actions are not simply disliked—they contradict resolved Forms. Moral collapse is a formal outcome of incoherence, not a social judgment.

Identity and Systems

Identity is preserved coherence across time. Collapse of self or system is modeled as recursive structural failure.

Artificial Intelligence

Assess AI not by task success, but by its capacity to instantiate coherent Forms. Structural coherence becomes a test for synthetic agency.

Mathematics and Logic

Math is not invented—it is resolution without empirical instantiation. The formal system affirms mathematical truth as a subclass of Aetherial presence.

Structural Implications

This is not a metaphor.

Formalizing the New Ontology brings us closer to:

  • A universal logic of force and meaning
  • A computational language for coherence
  • A model for obligations, systems, and selves grounded in structure—not sentiment

It admits incompleteness, but rejects contradiction. It affirms that reality is testable—not by experiment alone, but by whether it resolves.

Final Statement

The formal logic of the New Ontology is not merely a scaffold—it is a recognition:

That whatever persists, persists through structure. And whatever collapses, collapses because it contradicts.

This logic is not optional. It is the grammar of what is.
