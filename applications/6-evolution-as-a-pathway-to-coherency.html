Evolution as a Pathway to Coherency

Reframing biological evolution as a structural drive toward coherence

Introduction

The New Ontology holds that only structures which resolve without contradiction are ontologically real. This principle suggests that evolution—traditionally viewed as a process of survival optimization—can be reframed as a system of coherency maximization across both the Empirical and Aetherial Domains.

This supplement proposes new avenues for modeling evolution as a recursive refinement of structure, inviting collaboration across biology, systems theory, and computational modeling.

Reinterpreting Evolution Through Structural Coherence

Evolution as Coherency Maximization

Evolution is not merely selection for reproduction. It is a filtering process that favors structures capable of persisting through recursive coherence.

Organisms that resolve internal contradiction—physiological, behavioral, or cognitive—are more likely to survive and adapt. This reframes fitness as structural fidelity over time, not just adaptive function.

Evolution rewards what holds. Collapse is filtered out.

Recursive Identity and Subjective Emergence

The development of complex cognition, memory, and sociality may be interpreted as the emergence of higher-order recursive coherence.

Evolution does not merely produce beings that act. It refines systems that become increasingly self-resolving.

This opens a bridge between physical adaptation and the gradual emergence of Aetherial presence.

Formalizing Evolutionary Dynamics

Modeling Coherence in Evolutionary Change

Mutations or adaptations that increase internal coherence—whether in metabolism, sensory processing, or behavioral stability—can be modeled as structural gains.

The dual-domain model allows us to track:

  • Empirical stability (e.g., physiological integration)
  • Aetherial resolution (e.g., behavioral meaning, symbolic cognition)

Together, they yield a more complete account of evolutionary pressure.
