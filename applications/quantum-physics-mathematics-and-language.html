Quantum Physics, Mathematics, and Language

Exploring Interfaces Between Empirical Regularity and Structured Meaning

Introduction

The New Ontology defines reality as the dual resolution of force-governed and meaning-governed structure. Presence is not granted by perception, but by coherence across constraint.

This supplement applies the structural principles of the New Ontology to three distinct but convergent fields:

  • Quantum physics
  • Mathematics
  • Language and semiotics

Each of these disciplines presents a case where structure is present, but not always understood as such. By applying structural coherence as the test of being, we dissolve old dualisms and provide a new frame for integration.

Quantum Physics: Structural Resolution in the Microcosm

Superposition and Collapse

Quantum superposition is not indeterminacy—it is structurally valid possibility under constraint. Collapse is not caused by measurement. It is the system resolving to the single configuration that survives contradiction.

Quantum behavior does not violate order. It expresses constraint under resolution.

This reframing recasts quantum mechanics as a domain where coherence selects outcome, not chance.

Noncontradiction as Quantum Order

Quantum coherence is not abstract. It reflects the real structural requirement that no outcome may instantiate if it contradicts resolved Forms.

Wavefunction collapse becomes structural pruning. Measurement is not intrusion—it is structural registration.

The New Ontology treats linguistic systems as mappings—some better than others—onto real meaning-bearing Forms.

A statement is valid not because it is believed, but because it coheres.

Language becomes a mirror for structural constraint, not a theater of invention.
