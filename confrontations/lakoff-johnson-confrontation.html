Confrontation: Lakoff & Johnson

Embodied Cognition vs. Structural Independence

I. Thinkers

George Lakoff & Mark Johnson — Known for pioneering the field of embodied cognition and for arguing that metaphor is not a mere linguistic flourish, but a fundamental mechanism of thought.

II. Central Philosophical Claim Challenged

They contend that all concepts—including those traditionally regarded as abstract—are constructed through embodied, metaphorically grounded cognitive structures. Meaning is not derived from universal truths but emerges from neural mappings shaped by bodily experience. Even logic and morality, they claim, are metaphoric extrapolations from physical constraints.

III. Claimed Consequences

If true, then structural universality is an illusion; what we call “truth” is merely the residue of cultural metaphor, encoded through bodily experience. All understanding, even mathematics or ethics, is the local result of embodied schema—not access to objective Forms.

IV. Structural Conflict

Lakoff and Johnson mistake the route to a Form for the Form itself. It is undeniably true that embodied cognition mediates how humans conceptualize reality. But this describes a feature of epistemic access, not ontological structure. The metaphor “up is good” may aid in our grasp of moral coherence, but it is not constitutive of moral coherence. Forms exist independently of the means by which we arrive at them; structural meaning is not created by embodiment—it is resolved through it. To conflate embodied metaphor with metaphysical ground is to collapse the distinction between the finger and the moon it points to.

V. Reconciliation Attempt

Lakoff and Johnson’s contribution is best understood not as a theory of ontology, but as a theory of interpretive scaffolding. Within the New Ontology, we affirm that embodiment shapes interpretive strategies. But we reject the inference that this shaping entails construction. One may reach toward gravity through myth, metaphor, or measurement—but gravity is not made of the tools used to discover it. The same is true of moral or conceptual Forms. Their truth is not invalidated by their route of access. Cultural contingency explains distortion, not relativism.

VI. Verdict

  • Valid insight: Embodied cognition shapes access, not essence.
  • Invalid extension: Bodily mediation does not entail structural contingency.
  • Philosophical risk: Mistakes interpretive metaphor for ontological foundation.
  • Resolution: Preserve their insights within a clarified two-domain framework—where embodiment influences the seeker, not the sought.
