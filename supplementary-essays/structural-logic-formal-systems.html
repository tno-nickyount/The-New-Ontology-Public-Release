Structural Logic of Formal Systems

I. Introduction: Logic Precedes Symbol

In the prevailing view, logic is synonymous with symbolic formalism: systems of signs manipulated according to fixed rules. But within The New Ontology, logic precedes symbol. Logic is structural recursion integrity—the measure of whether reality coheres across the force-governed empirical domain and the meaning-governed aetherial domain.

Symbols help describe this structure, but they do not govern it. Structural logic is the grammar of reality itself—the rules by which recursion holds or collapses, independent of the representational systems we use to map it.

II. Defining Structural Logic: Coherence as Recursion Integrity

Reality, as defined in The New Ontology, is the outcome of the coherent resolution of force-governed empirical objects (Eᵒ) and meaning-governed aetherial forms (Fᵐ):

Reality ≡ R(Eᵒ ⊗ Fᵐ)

Where:

  • R: Recursion integrity—the measure of structural coherence.
  • Eᵒ: Empirical objects governed by forces.
  • Fᵐ: Aetherial forms governed by meaning.
  • ⊗: The and/with operator, representing the inseparable linkage between empirical and aetherial constituents.

Structural logic determines whether recursion holds or collapses. It is the superordinate logic governing experience as a coherent unfolding across time.

III. Collapse as Structural Incoherence: Not Prediction Failure

In traditional systems, logical failure means a contradiction among symbols or propositions. But in The New Ontology, collapse is not contradiction—it is structural incoherence between domains.

Collapse occurs when recursion integrity fails:

  • Empirical collapse: matter ceases to obey its force dynamics (e.g., disintegration).
  • Aetherial collapse: meaning collapses into incoherence (e.g., contradiction, psychosis).
  • Recursive collapse: the agent ceases to experience as a coherent entity (e.g., death).

Collapse ≠ prediction failure—a model may fail to predict yet reality persists. Collapse is coherence failure itself, not a representational mistake.

IV. Formal Systems as Subdomains of Structural Logic

Formal systems—such as mathematics, physics equations, classical logic, and symbolic logic—are subordinate representations within structural logic. They are tools for describing recursion integrity, not the structure itself:

  • Mathematics: aetherial recursion without empirical instantiation.
  • Physics equations: empirical recursion under force dynamics.
  • Classical logic: maps propositional relationships using truth-functional operators.
  • Symbolic logic: manipulates signs to model structural relationships.

These systems represent recursion—they do not constitute it. Structural logic is the superordinate coherence across all systems. It governs whether formal systems cohere with reality.

V. Coherence Across Descriptive Layers: Rhetoric, Symbol, Structure

Reality can be described across layers:

  • Rhetorical: "Reality is the outcome of the coherent resolution of force-governed empirical objects and meaning-governed aetherial forms."
  • Symbolic shorthand: Reality ≡ R(Eᵒ ⊗ Fᵐ)
  • Structural logic: the recursion integrity itself—whether coherence holds in reality.

Each layer is a translation of recursion—none replaces the others.

VI. Conclusion: Logic as the Measure of Coherence

Logic, within The New Ontology, is structural recursion integrity—the measure of whether coherence holds across force and meaning:

  • Does recursion cohere or collapse?
  • Do empirical dynamics and aetherial forms hold together across time?

Formal systems—math, physics, classical logic—are maps, not terrain.

Structural logic is the grammar of recursion—the fundamental logic of reality.
